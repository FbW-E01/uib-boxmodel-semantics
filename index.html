What's immersive theatre ?

How is immersive theatre different?

Many people go to the theatre to be transported into another world. However, no kind of theatre transports an audience quite like immersive theatre. In immersive theatre, the audience are not merely passive bystanders. They are part of the story, and they are in the middle of the action.

Source: Space.org.uk

What makes immersive theatre a great experience?

When done right productions focus on these six elements:

  • creates a physical environment that differs from a traditional theater where audiences sit in seats and watch a show unfurl on a stage with a curtain
  • tends to stimulate all five senses, not just sight and sound
  • doubles as an art installation and hands-on museum
  • makes individual audience members feel as if they have had a uniquely personal experience, that they are not just part of the crowd
  • often emphasize social interactions, either through directed tasks in small groups, or by fostering a looser party atmosphere
  • has a story to tell—and gives respect to storytelling

Source: New York Theatre

Famous immersive theatre productions

Sleep No More (Punchdrunk Theatre, New York 2011)

Sleep No More is Punchdrunk Theater’s staging of Macbeth, as if retold by Alfred Hitchcock and Isadora Duncan. It has been running since 2011 in a formerly abandoned club in Chelsea renamed the McKittrick Hotel.

Source: Review

Then She Fell (Third Rail Projects, New York 2012)

Those looking to unlock the secret to the success of Then She Fell, the Third Rail Projects’ immersive take on Lewis Carroll and his writings now entering its fourth year, might start with the old-fashioned set of keys each member of the audience is given at the start of our adventure through this theatrical Wonderland.

Source: Review
